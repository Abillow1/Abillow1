It is my belief that true innovation thrives with the free, unhindered flow of knowledge. Additionally, I believe that difficult and stressful times also fuel innovation. Throughout history we have seen innovation booms when this happens, and innovation stalls when it doesn't. Not to mean innovation cannot happen outside of this, but that this has a clear and direct impact on the speed, quality, and direction of innovation. 

I believe that it is only with this free and unhindered flow of knowledge that meaningful innovation can come from the common Joe. It is only with this flow of knowledge that people, regardless of background, are able to educate themselves for free. It is only with this ability that people are able to connect and make communities that innovate. With these communities that people make, they innovate on anything that they take interest to, and this contributes into the free and unhindered flow of knowledge. It's truly beautiful. 

It is with the internet that this has truly exploded. Now, communities are able to be assembled of people from anywhere, at any time, and the members of said community are able to contribute on their own time. This has led to an innovation boom in modern times. Just look on Github, Kickstarter, or really any other platform and you will see new projects and communities constantly. All of these projects and communities fuel further innovation and contribute to this free and unhindered flow of knowledge. Its a cycle, and a beautiful one at that. 

With this, I encourage you to tinker, to look into things, instead of appreciating the things around you, wonder how they work. Tap in to this beautiful flow of knowledge, learn and educate yourself. Join a community, try contributing to it! Mistakes will be made, and that's good! A mistake is true proof of innovation; without them, no innovation is happening. And who knows, some of today's most notable items are products of mistakes, maybe your mistake will open a new door! 

Never stop questioning, never stop wondering, never stop innovating. Go wild.
